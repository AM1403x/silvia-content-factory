Tesla Down 60%? Inside the JPMorgan Note That Could Reshape the Mag 7 Trade

JPMorgan published a research note Tuesday warning that Tesla (TSLA) could fall as much as 60 percent from current levels, with the stock closing Friday around $360 after a Q1 delivery miss of 358,000 against a consensus of 366,000. That's a sell-side bank putting a number on the downside that most of Wall Street has been too polite to write down.

If you own TSLA, the right question isn't whether JPMorgan is right. It's whether you have a thesis that survives being publicly told you're wrong.

What JPMorgan Actually Said

The note doesn't bury the lead. It models out three scenarios for Tesla's 2026 and 2027 earnings, and in the bear case the stock trades down to roughly $144, which is the 60 percent drawdown figure that got the tape moving Tuesday morning. The base case isn't much friendlier, with a price target that implies 25 to 30 percent downside from Friday's close. Only the bull case keeps you whole, and the bull case requires the robotaxi business to be generating real revenue by mid-2027.

Here's the thing about tier-1 sell-side downgrades with big downside numbers. Banks don't publish a 60 percent downside scenario without running it past risk, compliance, and the head of equity research first. By the time it's in your inbox, they've already derisked the call internally and spoken to their biggest clients. The retail investor is the last one to see it.

The Delivery Miss and the Analyst Pile-On

Tesla's Q1 delivery number was 358,000 vehicles against a sell-side consensus of 366,000 and a buy-side whisper closer to 375,000. Production came in at 408,000, which means Tesla built 50,000 more cars than it sold in the quarter. That's the delivery-production gap, and it's the cleanest tell for demand weakness you'll ever get from a car company.

A 50,000-vehicle gap doesn't just hit the revenue line. It sits on the balance sheet as finished-goods inventory, which means Tesla either has to cut prices to move it (hurting margin) or hold it (hurting working capital). Both outcomes show up in the Q1 financial print on April 22, and neither is priced into the stock at $360.

The JPMorgan note wasn't the only analyst action Tuesday. Citi trimmed AMD, BofA downgraded Carvana, and JPMorgan separately raised Merck. That pattern of mixed-to-negative action across growth and consumer names tells you this isn't an isolated TSLA opinion. It's coordinated positioning.

The Energy Storage Problem Nobody's Pricing

Tesla deployed only 8.8 GWh of energy storage in Q1, down 38 percent from the Q4 record of 14.2 GWh. That's a huge miss in a segment the bulls have been calling Tesla's next $10 billion business. Megapack demand was supposed to be the hedge against slowing auto sales, and in Q1 it went the wrong way.

The company will blame the lumpy project timing that characterizes utility-scale storage. That's fair, but the reason storage was supposed to be interesting was its margin profile, not its unit count. If you can't hit the unit count, the margin contribution story gets pushed out another year. That's another leg of the bull thesis on a wobbly stool.

The Mag 7 Re-Rating and What to Do If You Own It

Year to date, the Russell 2000 is outperforming the Nasdaq-100 by about 8 percentage points. That's the kind of rotation you see when investors start believing the AI megacap trade has peaked and broadening matters more than concentration. Tesla has been one of the worst performers in the Mag 7, down 20 percent YTD into Tuesday's note, with Apple and Alphabet roughly flat and Nvidia holding most of its gains.

If TSLA breaks the $340 support level it held through late March, the next technical stop is $305. Below that, the door opens to the $270 area where the stock last traded cleanly in 2024. A 60 percent drawdown from current levels is the bear case, but even the base case puts you at $270. That's not tail risk. That's the mid-probability scenario.

I'm not going to tell you to sell. I'll tell you what the position requires to make sense. You need either a strong conviction view on the robotaxi business timing, or you need to treat TSLA as a trading position with a defined stop. What doesn't work is "I've owned it for five years and it always comes back." That's not a thesis. That's an emotional attachment to a stock that no longer rewards it the way it did from 2020 to 2021.

Sizing matters here. If TSLA is 8 percent or more of your portfolio, the Q1 print on April 22 is a binary event you have to decide about in advance. Either cut down to 4 percent and hold the rest into the number, or go flat and re-enter after the reaction. Holding a max-size position into a print the Street is already negative on is the worst of both worlds.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is JPMorgan's 60 percent target their official price target?

It's the bear case scenario in a three-scenario model, not the headline PT. The base case implies roughly 25 to 30 percent downside, and the bull case has the stock higher. But the fact that JPMorgan is willing to publish $144 as a modeled outcome is what moved sentiment Tuesday.

When does Tesla report Q1 2026 earnings?

April 22 after the close. Deliveries are already out, so the print will be about automotive gross margin ex-credits, energy storage deployment, and any language on robotaxi progress. Watch the guidance on FY26 unit delivery trajectory. That's the number that moves the stock.

Does the JPMorgan call change the Mag 7 trade?

It reinforces a rotation that was already happening. The Russell 2000 is outpacing the Nasdaq-100 YTD, which tells you investors have been quietly broadening exposure. A public TSLA downgrade gives them permission to accelerate that move without feeling like they're calling a top.

What's the production-delivery gap and why does it matter?

It's the difference between how many cars Tesla built in the quarter and how many it delivered to customers. A 50,000-unit gap is large by historical standards and signals softer demand, which typically forces price cuts, financing promotions, or inventory write-downs in the following quarter.

Are other Mag 7 names at risk of similar notes?

Apple has been flagged for iPhone unit risk, and Meta's ad-revenue growth is decelerating. But neither has the scenario exposure Tesla does because their bear cases are smaller downside percentages. TSLA is unique in that the stock still trades at a multiple that prices in a future business that hasn't started generating meaningful revenue.
