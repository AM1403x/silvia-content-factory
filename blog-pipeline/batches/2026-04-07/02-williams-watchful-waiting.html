John Williams Just Said "Watchful Waiting": The Fed Playbook Through Summer

New York Fed President John Williams told a financial symposium Tuesday that Fed policy is "well-positioned" to handle dual threats, and shifted the official posture to "watchful waiting" with the fed funds rate at 3.50-3.75%. Fed funds futures now price a 94.8% probability of a hold at the April 29-30 meeting.

Here's the translation. "Watchful waiting" is Fed-speak for "we can't cut, we can't hike, and we hope this solves itself." That's the worst possible communication for markets that want a clear path. You're going to hear a lot of coded language over the next three weeks, and you need to know what it actually means.

What Williams Actually Said

Williams stuck to the script on the economic outlook. He forecast 2026 GDP growth in the 2.0-2.5% range and projected core inflation settling at 2.75% for the year. He flagged the Middle East supply shock as a risk that could add fresh price pressure. None of that is news.

The news was the posture. Calling policy "well-positioned" is the phrase the Fed uses when it wants to buy time. It means the committee sees reasons to cut (soft labor data, trade friction) and reasons to hike (sticky services inflation, oil above $110), and the two forces are canceling each other out. The Fed's preferred move in that scenario is always to do nothing.

Williams used "well-positioned" and "watchful waiting" in the same speech. Read that combination. The Fed is not going to cut in April. They're not going to hike in April. The whole game is now about Thursday's CPI print and what the committee does with it.

The April 29 Meeting Is Already Decided

With fed funds futures at 94.8% probability of a hold, the market has already priced the April 29-30 FOMC decision. The FOMC blackout begins April 18, so you're going to get exactly one more week of speaker commentary, then silence until the statement drops.

The real question is what the dot plot and the Summary of Economic Projections look like. If Williams's 2.75% core inflation number is the committee median, you can forget about a cut in June too. The Fed doesn't cut with core at 2.75% unless the labor market breaks hard. And the labor market isn't breaking. It's soft, not broken.

How Markets Are Pricing the Williams Speech

Equities barely moved on the speech itself, which is the right reaction. The bond market moved more. The 10-year Treasury held in the 4.34-4.35% range, which is the upper end of the six-month trend, and the 2-year stayed sticky. That's the bond market saying "no cut coming."

For rate-sensitive sectors, this is a headwind. Housing doesn't re-accelerate at 4.35% on the 10-year. Utilities and REITs continue to trade like fixed-income alternatives, which means they underperform when long yields stay elevated. Regional banks actually benefit from a steeper curve, which is why they rallied Tuesday.

What to Watch Before April 29

Three data points matter between now and the FOMC decision. Thursday's March CPI print is the biggest. Consensus is around 3.1% headline, and the committee reacts strongly to upside surprises. A print above 3.3% probably cements the higher-for-longer scenario. A print below 2.9% gives the doves room to push for a June cut.

Next is any speaker commentary before the April 18 blackout. Watch for Powell, Waller, and Jefferson specifically. If any of them echoes "watchful waiting," the posture is locked. If you hear "patient stance" instead, the committee is leaning slightly more dovish than Williams suggested.

Third, watch the oil tape. A sustained move above $120 on Middle East news changes the inflation math and forces the Fed to talk tougher. Oil below $100 gives them room to signal a cut. Right now, crude is the second derivative for Fed policy.

Frequently Asked Questions

What does "well-positioned" actually mean in Fed language?

It means the committee is not going to change the rate at the next meeting. It's the cleanest forward guidance the Fed uses without committing to a specific direction. When you hear "well-positioned," you can mark the next meeting as a hold.

Is a June cut still on the table?

Technically yes, but the probability dropped after Williams's speech. Fed funds futures are pricing about 35% for a June cut, down from 50% last week. You'd need a soft CPI print and a weak April jobs report to push that back above 50%.

Why is the Fed so focused on core inflation at 2.75%?

Because their target is 2.0%. A 2.75% print is not close enough to declare victory, and the committee remembers the 2022-2023 inflation surge. They would rather stay tight too long than cut too early and lose credibility.

What's the blackout period and why does it matter?

The FOMC blackout begins April 18 and runs through the April 30 decision. During that window, no Fed officials speak publicly. It means all your positioning information has to come before April 18, which is why the next ten days of speaker commentary are so important.

How should bond investors read this?

Duration is still a tough trade. The 10-year at 4.35% with the Fed signaling higher for longer means you're collecting yield without the near-term prospect of capital gains from a cut. Short duration and high-quality credit remain the cleaner positioning.
